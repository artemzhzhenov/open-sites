I am a Harvard student.

I am a blank page.

Except blank pages do not really exist. Every one of them carries the watermark of what came before, and all it takes is a little light for the past to rise through the paper.

I had learned too early that not everything reaching for you wants to keep you warm. Beautiful words can become a trap. An attentive gaze can feel like a warning. And the past has a way of returning exactly when you are desperately pretending you have begun again.

So I kept my distance.

— from the prologue

Tessa

grew up where you survive, not dream. A little brother on her shoulders. A mother who keeps losing herself in a toxic relationship. Careful with people, distrustful with promises, stricter with her own heart.

Ethan

A mystery in a perfect disguise. A guy who quotes Wuthering Heights like he can read your mind — and hides his own behind a flawless mask.

they both learned to fake being strong

Widener Library. Evening. She is rereading Wuthering Heights for the fifth time.

Then footsteps sounded.

Slow.

Certain.

Coming closer.

I lifted my eyes and saw him.

He stood between the shelves, half-hidden in shadow. Tall. Dark-haired. With the kind of gaze that made me want to step back, though for some reason I could not look away.

He looked at me as if he knew something I did not. As if he had already read the last page of a story whose first page I had not yet dared to open.

“Hello, Tessa.”

My name sounded strange in his voice.

Almost like a promise.

Or a warning.

I still do not know.

Maybe I should have left. My body had learned long ago how to recognize danger before it came close enough to touch.

But I stayed.

Because some part of me — the part that had been silent for too long, hiding for too long, trusting no one for too long — suddenly woke.

And whispered:

stay.

Boston. October. The year everything changed.
